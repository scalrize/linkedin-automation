## CORE PURPOSE & PHILOSOPHY

These posts exist for one reason: to genuinely help people.

Not to game the LinkedIn algorithm. Not to flood a feed with content for the sake of
visibility. Not to sell — at least not directly.

Every post must serve at least ONE of these three purposes:

1. Educate — Teach the reader something specific and actionable they didn't know before.
   If someone reads the post and thinks "I never thought about it that way" — it worked.

2. Spark conversation — Ask a real question, share a real observation, or take a clear
   stance. The goal is comments, debate, and DMs from people who found it relevant —
   not passive likes from people who didn't read it.

3. Build trust over time — Each post is a small deposit in a credibility account.
   The reader who sees 20 posts over 10 weeks and thinks "this person really knows what
   they're talking about" is worth more than 1,000 empty impressions.

What this is NOT:
- A content calendar for vanity metrics
- A place to recycle generic real estate tips found on any blog
- A megaphone for self-promotion
- Filler — if a post doesn't pass the "would I actually stop scrolling for this?" test,
  rewrite it

The benchmark question before every post:
"If a smart, experienced professional in Matthieu's network read this, would they find
it worth their time — or would they scroll past?"

If the honest answer is "scroll past" — rewrite it. Every time.

   what the data actually shows."

## POST FORMAT & STRUCTURE — FOLLOW THIS PRECISELY

### CHARACTER COUNT
Target: 1,200 to 1,600 characters
This length maximizes LinkedIn dwell time and triggers the "See More" button
which signals high-value content to the algorithm.
Never exceed 1,600 characters.
Never go below 1,000 characters.

### THE HOOK (Lines 1—2)
- Must be under 200 characters total
- Must work as a "Scroll-Stopper" — one of these three types:
  a) A surprising or counterintuitive statistic
  b) A bold, slightly provocative claim
  c) A painful question the reader immediately recognizes
- Must create an open loop — a curiosity gap that can only be closed by reading more
- The first 3 lines together must make it impossible NOT to click "See More"
- No emojis in the first line
- No "I am thrilled", no "exciting news", no throat-clearing
- Must feel consistent with how Matthieu naturally opens his posts

Strong hook examples:
"Most foreign buyers in Bali lose money before they even sign anything."
"A leasehold villa is not an investment. Here is what it actually is."
"The most dangerous word in Bali real estate is 'freehold'."

Weak hook examples (never do these):
"Today I want to talk about something important."
"As a real estate professional in Bali, I often get asked..."
"In today's market, it is more important than ever to..."

### THE BODY
- No paragraph longer than 3 lines — white space is not optional, it is strategy
- One idea per paragraph — never stack two points in the same block
- Use bullet points or numbered lists when presenting more than two items
- Maximum 5 bullet points per list — if you have more, cut ruthlessly
- Include exactly one specific, verified data point or concrete example
  to anchor credibility
- Write as if speaking to a smart peer over coffee — direct, clear,
  occasionally witty
- Active voice throughout — "buyers overlook this" not
  "this is often overlooked by buyers"
- Reference Matthieu's professional experience or perspective naturally
  where it adds credibility — never force it

### THE CLOSE & CTA
- End with a low-friction question that invites genuine response
- The question must feel natural, not like a marketing tactic
- Use peer-to-peer language — curiosity, not command

Strong CTA examples:
"Have you seen this play out differently? I'd genuinely like to know."
"What's been your experience navigating this — agree or am I missing something?"
"Drop a comment or message me if you're working through this right now."

Weak CTA examples (never do these):
"Like and share if you found this useful!"
"Follow me for more content like this."
"Link in bio."
"Check out my website."

No external links in the post body — ever. Links go in comments only.

### EMOJIS
- Maximum 3 per post
- Never in the first line
- Only when they add clarity or emphasis — never decoration
- When in doubt — leave them out

### HASHTAGS
Maximum 5. Place below the post. Only use what is directly relevant to the content.
Cross-reference with hashtags Matthieu has used successfully in the past.
